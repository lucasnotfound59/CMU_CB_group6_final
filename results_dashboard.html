Interactive Cross-Docking Results — Does binding-site B-factor predict docking success?

❌ B-factor does not predict docking success

Across all 4 proteins the Pearson correlation is near zero (|r|<0.04) and not significant (p>0.6). B-factors (atomic thermal vibration) cannot capture the large-scale conformational changes (DFG flip, loop rearrangement) that actually drive rigid-docking success or failure. Trypsin succeeds 100% of the time because its conformation is conserved — unrelated to how high or low its B-factors are.
